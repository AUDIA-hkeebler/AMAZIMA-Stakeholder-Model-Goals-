Goal Setting
That Can Be Measured

In the Stakeholder Model, strong goals are built with objective sub-tasks so each person can complete a clear monthly 1-10 self-assessment. Performance drives recognition, so goals must be specific, time-bound, and evidence-based.

01 / Foundation

The Objective Goal Standard

Before finalizing any goal, ask: Can we point to a number and a piece of evidence? If the task has a clear count, clear timeline, and clear proof, monthly coaching becomes fair, simple, and consistent.

Vague

"Improve my attitude at work."

Stakeholder Objective

"Give three colleague shout-outs during weekly huddles each month to build a culture of appreciation."

02 / The Three Pillars

Balanced Goal Design

Mastery of Existing Skills (1/3)

Improve quality, efficiency, consistency, and stewardship in current responsibilities.

Good Goal Examples
  • Reduce report turnaround from 5 days to 3 by Month 4.
  • Maintain 100% attendance accuracy for the full segment.
  • Create and publish an SOP for weekly inventory by Month 3.

New Skill Development (1/3)

Grow capability through cross-training, projects, education, and leadership readiness.

Good Goal Examples
  • Complete Advanced Leadership module and submit certificate.
  • Shadow Finance 4 hours monthly for grant reporting.
  • Lead one 15-minute team training on new software.

Culture and Character (1/3)

Live values in visible ways that strengthen unity, service, and spiritual community.

Good Goal Examples
  • Lead morning devotion monthly.
  • Complete 3 cross-department encouragement coffees.
  • Implement one practical morale-strengthening action.

Objective Sub-Tasks in Practice

Example 1: Operations / Mentor

Pillar: Mastery of Existing Skills
Goal: Gold Standard efficiency and accuracy in student progress reporting.

  • File all 30 weekly check-in reports by Friday 5:00 PM.
    Timeline: Monthly | Evidence: 100% submission log
  • Maintain zero missing-data flags in monthly reporting.
    Timeline: Monthly | Evidence: Spreadsheet audit
  • Submit one critical growth story monthly for Donor Relations.
    Timeline: Months 1-6 | Evidence: 6 stories submitted

Example 2: Emerging Leader / Admin

Pillar: New Skill Development
Goal: Build competency in departmental budget management.

  • Complete Finance Basics module and pass final quiz.
    Timeline: By Month 2 | Evidence: Completion certificate
  • Attend two budget-to-actual review meetings with Finance Director.
    Timeline: Months 2-3 | Evidence: Attendance + notes
  • Prepare Month 5 and Month 6 draft budgets independently.
    Timeline: Months 5-6 | Evidence: Drafts 48h before deadline

Example 3: All Team Members

Pillar: Culture and Character
Goal: Strengthen morale and spiritual community in the Jinja office.

  • Lead staff devotion twice during the segment.
    Timeline: Months 1-6 | Evidence: Sign-up confirmation
  • Present one written solution for an admin friction point.

  • Hold one Character Coffee monthly with another department.
    Timeline: Monthly | Evidence: 6 meetings logged
04 / Objective Checklist

Test Every Sub-Task for Clarity

1. Evidence Test

  • If I tell my manager this is 100% complete, can I point to a physical document, a digital file, or a specific date on a calendar to prove it?
  • Does the sub-task avoid vague words like \"better,\" \"more,\" \"improve,\" or \"try\"?

2. Number Test

  • Does the sub-task include a specific count or percentage (e.g., \"4 meetings,\" \"10 reports,\" \"100% accuracy\")?
  • If I only do half the work, is it mathematically clear that my self-assessment score should be a 5?

3. Timeline Test

  • Does the sub-task have a clear deadline within this 6-month (or 5-month) sprint?
  • Is it clear which month the work needs to be started and finished?

4. Support Test

  • Have I identified what I need from my manager to hit a 10 on this goal?
  • Does this sub-task directly help meet one of the high-level Departmental Outcomes my manager provided?
